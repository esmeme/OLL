The Creation of the NOOC

Practical Implementation of Theoretical Principles


To implement the theoretical principles described in the Theoretical Background, we have designed a methodology that focuses on active student interaction with the course content. Some of the pedagogical strategies we will implement are:

  • Creating Korean letters like puzzle pieces: Inspired by the historical process of Hangul invention, we have designed lessons that allow students to intuitively understand the structure and logic of the Korean writing system.
  • Exploring consonants and vowels as pieces of a linguistic puzzle: Instead of presenting letters in isolation, we have taught Korean consonants and vowels as elements that combine to form syllables and words. This helps students understand the relationship between sounds and written symbols.
  • Focus on syllable formation and reading rules: At the end of the course, we highlight the importance of syllables and reading rules, showing how these follow logical patterns based on previously learned concepts. We provide students with practical tools to remember these rules and apply them in their daily reading and writing.
  • Applying phonetics to improve pronunciation: We use phonetic principles to help students improve their Korean pronunciation. Through practical exercises and listening activities, students develop a deeper understanding of Korean sounds and how to produce them correctly.
  • Promoting active and reflective learning: We encourage students' active participation in the learning process through practical activities, application exercises, and personal reflection. This allows them to internalize the material more effectively and apply what they have learned in real situations.

By integrating these strategies into the design and implementation of the course, we aim to create a dynamic and stimulating learning environment that promotes active engagement and student success.

Creation of the OER

Decision on the Teaching Structure of Hangul

Before beginning the design of the OER, we made key decisions on how to divide the teaching of Hangul into clear and progressive stages. We decided to structure the course into modules that address specific aspects of Hangul, such as vowels, consonants, and syllable groups, to facilitate step-by-step learning and ensure a solid understanding.

Integration of Linguistic Concepts

Once the basic structure of the course was established, we focused on how to integrate relevant linguistic concepts into the teaching of Hangul. We identified key phonetic and grammatical principles that students would need to understand to effectively master the Korean writing system.

Incorporation of Linguistic Concepts in Teaching

With the identified linguistic concepts, we developed strategies to effectively incorporate them into the teaching of Hangul. We used practical examples and interactive activities to help students relate linguistic concepts to Hangul writing and pronunciation, facilitating their understanding and application.

Ideas and Tricks

During the design process, we relied on various ideas and tricks to enhance the effectiveness of the OER. This included using engaging visual resources, such as infographics and explanatory videos, to reinforce the concepts taught. We also implemented active learning techniques, such as memory games and practical exercises, to maintain student engagement and encourage active participation in learning Hangul.

Gradual Construction of Concepts

Starting from scratch, our approach focused on gradually building Hangul concepts on a solid foundation. We began with the basic fundamentals of the writing system and then progressed to more advanced concepts, such as word formation and Korean grammar. This gradual progression allowed students to develop a comprehensive and cohesive understanding of Hangul throughout the course.

Integration of Concepts for Comprehensive Understanding

Finally, we ensured that all taught concepts were seamlessly integrated to provide a comprehensive understanding of Hangul. By integrating different aspects of the language, from phonetics to grammar, we were able to offer students a complete and well-rounded learning experience that prepared them to effectively communicate in Korean.

Conclusion

In summary, our methodological approach to designing the OER for teaching Hangul was based on carefully considered decisions about the course structure, integration of linguistic concepts, practical application of these concepts, use of effective ideas and tricks, and gradual construction of knowledge for a comprehensive understanding of the Korean language.

This approach allowed us to develop, we believe, a solid and effective educational resource that prepares students to master Hangul meaningfully.
